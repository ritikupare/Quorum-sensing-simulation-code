Explore how bacteria decide to switch quorum sensing signaling ON or OFF based on local autoinducer concentration and temperature.

How it works

Bacteria produce an autoinducer signal molecule into the environment. When local concentration passes a threshold, bacteria turn quorum sensing signaling ON.

Temperature shifts the threshold and production rates: warmer conditions can promote signaling activation, while cooler conditions make activation harder.
